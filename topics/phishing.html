Phishing & Social Engineering

The tricks behind fake emails and texts, and the red flags that give them away before you click.

What phishing is

Phishing is a message that pretends to be from someone you trust — a bank, a coworker, a delivery service — in order to trick you into handing over information or clicking a malicious link. It's a form of social engineering: attacking people's trust instead of a computer's defenses.

Common red flags

Signs a message might be phishing
Red flag What to check
Mismatched sender Does the email address actually match the real company's domain?
Urgent language "Act now or your account will be suspended" is designed to rush you
Unexpected links or attachments Hover over links first; don't open attachments you weren't expecting
Requests to bypass normal steps A "manager" asking you to skip verification is a classic pretext

What to do instead

  • Go to the company's site directly rather than clicking the link in the message.
  • Verify unusual requests through a second channel, like a phone call.
  • Report suspicious messages to your email provider or IT department.
